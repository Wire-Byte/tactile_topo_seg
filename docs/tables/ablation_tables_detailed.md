# TP Segmentation Ablation Tables (Core + Weight)

## Experiment Setup (Common)
- Backbone network: SegFormer-B2 (MIT-B2 encoder), inherited from MMSeg SegFormer base config.
- Main segmentation head: SegFormer decode head with 2 classes (background / tactile_paving).
- Auxiliary branch: custom skeleton head `TPSkeletonHeadClDice`.
- Training schedule: iter-based training, `max_iters=10000`, `val_interval=500`.
- Dataset: `TPDataset` with `MapTPLabels` and `GenerateTPSkeleton` in pipeline.
- Metrics: mIoU / mDice / mFscore / mPrecision / mRecall from IoUMetric.

## Network Configuration Details
### 1) Main (decode) loss design
Main head uses three-term loss:
- `CrossEntropyLoss` with weight `1.0`
- `DiceLoss` with weight `0.5`
- `SoftCLDiceLoss` with tunable weight $\lambda_{main}$

### 2) Auxiliary head design and insertion position
Auxiliary head type:
- `TPSkeletonHeadClDice` (file: `mmseg_ext/models/tp_skeleton_head_clDice.py`)
- Structure: `Conv3x3(in_channels -> 64)` + `Conv1x1(64 -> 1)`
- Supervision target: `gt_skeleton` generated by `GenerateTPSkeleton`

Insertion position (actual ablation base in V2):
- `in_index=1`, `in_channels=128`, `channels=64`
- This corresponds to a deeper SegFormer feature stage than the original V1 setting (`in_index=0`, `in_channels=64`).

Auxiliary loss terms:
- `loss_skel_bce` (`CrossEntropyLoss`, sigmoid, weight `0.5`)
- `loss_skel_dice` (`DiceLoss`, sigmoid, weight `0.5`)
- `loss_skel_cldice` (`SoftCLDiceLoss`, foreground index 0, tunable weight $\lambda_{aux}$)

### 3) Total loss expression
The effective objective can be written as:

$$
\mathcal{L} =
\underbrace{\mathcal{L}_{CE}^{main} + 0.5\,\mathcal{L}_{Dice}^{main} + \lambda_{main}\,\mathcal{L}_{clDice}^{main}}_{\text{decode head}}
+
\underbrace{0.5\,\mathcal{L}_{BCE}^{aux} + 0.5\,\mathcal{L}_{Dice}^{aux} + \lambda_{aux}\,\mathcal{L}_{clDice}^{aux}}_{\text{skeleton aux head}}
$$

## Table A: Core Design Ablation (Main/Aux clDice on-off)
Source CSV: `docs/tables/ablation_table_core_components.csv`

| Case | Decode clDice | Aux clDice | mIoU | mDice | mPrecision | mRecall | Best Iter | Delta mIoU vs `core_none` |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| core_none | 0.0 | 0.0 | 86.47 | 92.47 | 92.78 | 92.16 | 10000 | 0.00 |
| core_main_only | 0.1 | 0.0 | 81.77 | 89.44 | 90.17 | 88.75 | 500 | -4.70 |
| core_aux_only | 0.0 | 0.1 | 83.74 | 90.75 | 89.75 | 91.84 | 9500 | -2.73 |
| core_both | 0.1 | 0.1 | **86.60** | **92.55** | 91.93 | **93.20** | 9500 | **+0.13** |

Interpretation:
- Enabling clDice on only one branch (`main` or `aux`) underperforms the no-clDice baseline in this run set.
- Jointly enabling clDice on both branches (`core_both`) gives the best mIoU among core variants.
- This supports a coupled topology prior: area/topology constraints work best when decode and skeleton branches are aligned.

## Table B: clDice Weight Sweep (Both branches tied)
Source CSV: `docs/tables/ablation_table_cldice_weights.csv`

| Case | $\lambda_{main}$ | $\lambda_{aux}$ | mIoU | mDice | mPrecision | mRecall | Best Iter | Delta mIoU vs `w_0p10` |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| w_0p05 | 0.05 | 0.05 | 83.94 | 90.88 | 89.90 | 91.95 | 10000 | -0.79 |
| w_0p10 | 0.10 | 0.10 | 84.73 | 91.38 | 91.15 | 91.61 | 6000 | 0.00 |
| w_0p20 | 0.20 | 0.20 | **85.73** | **92.01** | **91.44** | **92.60** | 8500 | **+1.00** |
| w_0p30 | 0.30 | 0.30 | 82.67 | 90.04 | 90.27 | 89.81 | 7000 | -2.06 |

Interpretation:
- The sweep shows a non-monotonic relationship with clDice strength.
- Moderate topology weight (`0.20`) performs best in this experiment set.
- Over-strong clDice (`0.30`) hurts both mIoU and recall, indicating over-constraint.
